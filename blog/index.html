Administrative Philosophy

December 6, 2018

In examining my administrative philosophy, I see four major threads emerging. First, that writing majors need to leverage and be adaptable to local contexts. Second, that they should be built on inquiry into the discipline of Rhetoric and Composition. Third, that they build on local expertise and needs. And fourth, that an effective writing major is built on praxis. This builds upon the idea that effective writing curriculum and pedagogy is responsive to and built out of local contexts, be responsive to local needs, and draw upon the interests and expertise of faculty, staff, and students within the university.

Teaching Philosophy

in 116

October 28, 2018

Old - To Update I view writing as the extension of thought into a physical form. Writing is not only able to convey information it also creates and changes meaning. Writing is in dialogue with the writer, the audience, and the broader context of culture and society. I want students to be able to enter into this dialogue as full participants, not just people who just consume and regurgitate the thoughts of others.
